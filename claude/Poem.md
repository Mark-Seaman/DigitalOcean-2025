# A Coder's Verse

The cursor blinks on an empty screen,
A blank white canvas, a world unseen.
With keystrokes soft and logic clear,
I build the things I hold most dear.

Functions bloom like morning flowers,
Variables counted through the hours.
A bug appears — I track it down,
Then wear the fix like a golden crown.

For in this craft of ones and zeros,
We are the quiet, unsung heroes,
Turning thought to living code,
Line by line along the road.
